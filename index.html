Kunjunni Mash

Poet, Lyricist



Kunjunni Mash at his ancestral house

Kunjunni (10 May 1927 - 26 March 2006), popularly known as Kunjunni Mash (Mash is the Malayalam equivalent of teacher), was an Indian poet of Malayalam literature. Known for his short poems with a philosophical overtone, his works were popular among the children as well as the grown-ups.

Kunjunni started his career as a teacher.He was an inmate of an Ashrama and taught, looked after and interacted with the hostel boys there. He retired from teaching in 1982 but continued to live in the Ashrama. He was known and admired for living a humble life with minimal needs, and few comforts or luxuries.

Kunjunni handled the column for children in the Mathrubhumi weekly under the pseudonym "Kuttettan". He initiated three or four generations of aspiring writers into writing.

He received several honours including three awards from the Kerala Sahitya Akademi and the lifetime achievement award of the State Institute of Children's Literature.

Kunjunni died in his ancestral house at Valapad on 26 March 2006.
